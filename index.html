01 — Overview

What Avero is

Avero is a personal security assistant for Android that detects phishing links, checks password strength, and monitors incoming messages for suspicious URLs. The core engine runs entirely on your device using a TFLite neural network trained on 530,000 real phishing URLs.

This Privacy Policy applies to the Avero Android application (com.mariusarzoiu.avero) published by NextByteStudios (Marius Arzoiu) and the associated website at avero.nextbytestudios.com.

✓ Privacy-first design
Your URLs, passwords, and SMS messages are never uploaded to any server. All risk analysis happens locally and the results remain on your device.

03 — SMS & Notifications

How SMS and notifications
are handled

Avero is classified as an Anti-SMS phishing application. The READ_SMS and RECEIVE_SMS permissions are used solely to scan incoming messages for malicious URLs.

  • SMS text content is read in memory, processed by the on-device model, and then discarded.
  • Only extracted URLs and their risk scores are stored locally in your scan history.
  • No SMS message body, sender information, or contact data is ever uploaded or shared.
  • The Notification Listener Service similarly extracts only URL strings from notification text — no app name, sender, or message content is retained.
  • You are shown a clear disclosure screen before Avero requests SMS or Notification Listener access, explaining exactly why each permission is needed.
⚠ You are in control
SMS and Notification Listener access can be revoked at any time in Android Settings → Apps → Avero → Permissions. Revoking these permissions disables auto-protection but all other features remain fully functional.
04 — Camera & QR

Camera access and
QR scanning

Avero uses the device camera exclusively for QR code scanning. When you open the QR scanner, the live camera feed is processed frame-by-frame on your device using Google ML Kit's Barcode Scanning library.

  • No photos or video are saved to your device or uploaded anywhere.
  • The camera is only active while the QR scanner screen is open and automatically stops when you leave.
  • When a QR code is detected, only the decoded URL string is passed to the phishing analysis engine — not the image frame.
  • Camera permission is optional. If denied, all other features continue to work normally.
ℹ On-device processing
QR decoding uses Google ML Kit running locally. No camera frames or decoded data are sent to Google or any other server as part of the Avero scanning flow.
05 — Firebase & Account

Firebase, accounts,
and cloud data

Avero uses Google Firebase for optional account features. No account is required to use link scanning, password checking, QR scanning, image OCR, or security courses — these all work fully offline.

If you choose to create an account, the following data is collected and stored in Firebase:

  • Firebase Authentication: Your email address and a hashed password (or OAuth token if using Google Sign-In). Used only for identity verification.
  • Firestore Database: App settings and preferences you choose to sync across devices. Scan history sync (if enabled). No SMS content, no passwords, no camera data.
  • Website waitlist: If you sign up on avero.nextbytestudios.com, your email is stored in Firestore solely to notify you of Pro plan availability.

Firebase services are provided by Google LLC and are subject to Google's Privacy Policy. Data is stored on Google Cloud infrastructure in the EU region where possible.

⚠ Google Play Services
Avero uses Google Play Services for distribution and basic functionality (Firebase, ML Kit). Google may collect device identifiers and diagnostic data according to their own privacy policy, independent of Avero.

07 — Local Storage

Data stored on your device

Avero stores the following data locally on your device using Android's Room database and DataStore:

  • Scan history: URLs you have scanned, their risk scores, verdicts, timestamps, and source (manual, SMS, notification, QR, image).
  • App preferences: Theme, notification settings, biometric lock preference.
  • Course progress: Which security lessons and quizzes you have completed.

All local data can be erased at any time by going to Android Settings → Apps → Avero → Storage → Clear Data, or by uninstalling the app.

08 — Network Access

When Avero
uses the internet

The core features of Avero — link scanning, password checking, QR scanning, and image OCR — work entirely offline. Network access is used only for the following optional or background functions:

  • Firebase Authentication and Firestore sync (only if you have an account).
  • Optional redirect-chain following (to check if a short URL leads to a known malicious domain). This can be disabled in settings.
  • Google Play Services background operations (handled by Android, not Avero).
09 — Third Parties

Third-party
services used

  • Google Firebase (Auth + Firestore) — for optional account features. Privacy policy →
  • Google ML Kit (Text Recognition, Barcode Scanning) — runs entirely on-device, no data sent to Google.
  • Google LiteRT / TensorFlow Lite — the phishing detection model runs locally, no data transmitted.
  • Google Play Services — for app distribution, crash reporting, and authentication. Subject to Google's privacy policy.

Avero contains no advertising SDKs and does not share data with analytics or marketing platforms.

10 — Children

Children's privacy

Avero is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the European Union). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data through Avero, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

11 — Security

How we protect
your data

Because the majority of Avero's processing is on-device, the attack surface for your personal data is minimal. For the data that does reach Firebase (email, preferences), we rely on Google Cloud's security infrastructure, including encryption at rest and in transit.

  • All Firebase communication is over HTTPS/TLS.
  • Firestore security rules restrict access so only authenticated users can read or write their own data.
  • No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we recommend not storing sensitive personal information in the app.
12 — Your Rights

Your data rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data. You can exercise these rights at any time:

  • Local data: Clear it via Android Settings → Apps → Avero → Storage → Clear Data.
  • Account data: Delete your account (see Section 13) or contact us to request a copy of your data.
  • Permissions: Revoke any Android permission at any time in device Settings without losing access to unrelated features.
  • Waitlist email: Reply to any waitlist email with "unsubscribe" and your data will be deleted within 48 hours.
13 — Account Deletion

How to delete
your account

If you have created an account in Avero, you have the right to permanently delete your account and all associated personal data stored in Firebase.

  • In-app: Go to Settings → Account → Delete Account. This immediately removes your Firebase Auth record and all Firestore data.
  • By email: Send a deletion request to nextbytestudioscontact@gmail.com with the email address linked to your account.
  • Deletion is permanent and completed within 7 days of verification. This action cannot be undone.
ℹ Google Play requirement
In compliance with Google Play's data deletion policy, this deletion option is also accessible at: avero.nextbytestudios.com/privacy-policy.html
